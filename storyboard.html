Proton Saga HMI Redesign — Rear Seat Child Presence Detection

The Scenario: How the Redesigned HMI Prevents the Incident

Amin, an experienced driver, places his 18-month-old daughter in the rear seat of his Proton Saga Premium MC3. Due to high cognitive load and a change in daily routine, he forgets she is there when arriving at work. This storyboard shows how each stage of the redesigned HMI intervenes to prevent tragedy.

Scene 1 — Morning
👨‍👧 🚗
Amin Places Daughter in Rear Seat
It's 7:45 AM. Due to a change in routine, Amin is dropping his daughter at daycare before work. He secures her in the child car seat on the rear-right seat.
HMI Response

⚖️ Rear-right pressure sensor activates — detects 10.2 kg. Dashboard displays: "Rear Passenger Detected" with a child seat icon. System enters monitoring mode.

Sensor Activated
Scene 2 — Driving
🚗 💨
Driving to Work — Routine Takes Over
Amin receives a stressful work call. His cognitive load increases. He misses the daycare turn and drives straight to the office on autopilot — a classic slip error due to routine capture.
HMI Response

Dashboard continues showing the rear passenger indicator. Bird's-eye cabin view displays the occupied rear-right seat in amber. System remains in passive monitoring.

Monitoring
Scene 3 — Arrival
🅿️ 🏢
Amin Parks at the Office
8:15 AM. Amin parks at his office. He turns off the engine and gathers his things. His daughter has fallen asleep silently in the back.
HMI Response

Engine OFF detected. Pressure sensor still reads 10.2 kg. System arms the exit-alert sequence and monitors the driver door.

Alert Armed
Scene 4 — The Critical Moment
🚶 🔑
Amin Opens the Driver Door to Exit
Amin opens the driver door and walks away. In the original Proton Saga, a faint chime plays once — easily missed. This is the moment the original system failed.
Original System (Failed)

❌ A single low-intensity "check your belongings" chime + small generic icon on the infotainment screen. Not prominent enough to break through cognitive tunnel vision.

Original System Failed Here
Scene 5 — Redesigned Stage 1
🔔 ⚠️
Loud Dashboard Warning + Chime Burst
The moment the driver door opens with a rear passenger still detected, the redesigned system triggers immediately.
Redesigned HMI — Stage 1

🔊 Loud, repeating 3-tone chime. Full-screen dashboard warning: "⚠ CHECK REAR SEAT — CHILD DETECTED" with flashing red background.

Escalation Stage 1
Scene 6 — Redesigned Stage 2 (30 sec)
📢 🚗
Horn Pulse Activates
30 seconds — no rear door opened. The system escalates.
Redesigned HMI — Stage 2

🚨 Horn pulses in SOS-like pattern. Hazard lights flash. Draws attention from Amin and surrounding people in the parking lot.

Escalation Stage 2
Scene 7 — Redesigned Stage 3 (60 sec)
📱 🌡️
Phone Alert + Temperature Warning
60 seconds — Amin is inside the building. Cabin temp rising to 38°C.
Redesigned HMI — Stage 3

📲 Push notification + SMS: "🚨 Your child is still in the vehicle. Cabin temp: 38°C. Return immediately." Reaches Amin even inside the building.

Escalation Stage 3
Scene 8 — Redesigned Stage 4 (120 sec)
🆘 🚑
Emergency Services Auto-Dial
2 minutes — no response. The system takes the final safety measure.
Redesigned HMI — Stage 4

📞 Auto-dials emergency services with GPS location. Windows crack open for airflow. AC continues on battery power.

Emergency — Final Stage
Scene 9 — Resolution
✅ 👶
Child Retrieved Safely
Amin sees the phone alert at Stage 3 and rushes back. He opens the rear door and takes his daughter. System detects weight change and deactivates.
HMI Response

✅ Rear-right sensor reads 0.0 kg. Dashboard: "Rear Seat Clear — Thank you for checking." All alerts cancelled. Event logged.

Incident Prevented

Why This Redesign Works — The Failures It Addresses

❌ Original Failure

Single low-intensity chime + generic "check belongings" icon — too subtle under cognitive load

✅ Redesigned Fix

4-stage escalation: loud chime → horn → phone alert → emergency call. Multi-modal alerts

❌ Original Failure

No way to reach driver once they've left — warning only existed inside the car

✅ Redesigned Fix

Stage 3 sends SMS + push notification to driver's phone even when away

❌ Original Failure

Physical obstruction — seat position and height blocked rear seat view

✅ Redesigned Fix

Pressure sensor + cabin display replaces line-of-sight with instrument detection
